Why Carrying a GPS Communicator is Essential

August 1, 2019 - Kolten Nay

A good reminder that these convenient devices can save lives and bring comfort to our loved ones.

GPS communicators are getting more popular among those I see backpacking and climbing by themselves. They are extremely handy pieces of technology, and some of the features that I use them for include:

  • 100% Global Iridium network keeps me connected anywhere I go
  • Weather Updates on Demand
  • SMS communication with anyone, including emergency responders
  • Location tracking intervals

Originally I picked up my Garmin inReach device to keep my parents off my back when I would take trips out of cell coverage by myself. I was able to send them messages checking in with my location, and tell them I was OK each night. I've never beein in a situation where I've had to use the SOS feature (thank god), but it allows for the user to communicate the exact nature and severity of their situation so the first responders can well, respond accordingly.

“We like companionship, see, but we can't stand to be around people for very long. So we go get ourselves lost, come back for a while, then get the hell out again.”

Jon Krakauer, Into the Wild

The above quote is one of my favorit from Into the Wild. It sums up a state that I derive happiness from - being alone. The rush of completing a trip by yourself, and only having your own thoughts 10,000ft up in the air on a mountain peak is sublime. I encourage everyone to seek these moments out, but to do that safely. Acquiring your own GPS communicators (I recommend Garmin) will allow you to do so without ostracizing yourself from the ones that you love. Happy adventuring, friends!
