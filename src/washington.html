The Evergreen State

Mt.Rainier

Description

Summiting Mt. Rainier is considered one of the greater mountaineering achievements in the 48 contiguous United States. The celebration regarding the peak is less about the length of the trek (14.7 miles, 8,986 ft), and more about the terrain in getting there. It's a 3 day summit climb up the Muir and Ingraham glaciers with 5,000ft of that elevation gain coming on the glacier! This is certainly a once in a lifetime trip.

Location

If you are attempting this trek, you shouldn't need telling where to go. Mt.Rainier is hard to miss, visible from the national park for hundreds of miles around. Stop at the Paradise Visitor Center for the easiest route up via Camp Muir and Dissapointment Cleaver.

Difficulty

EXPERT: This trek is only for individuals who have experience in glacier and high alpine travel, INCLUDING crevasse rescue. Crampons, ice axe, helmet, etc are basic necessities those daring enough. Grit enough won't get you top the alone, you need an experienced team as well. There are some wonderful local guide groups that are willing to take you under their wing for the right price as well.

Snow Lake

Description

Just a little more laid than Mt.Rainier is the trail to Snow Lake outside of Seattle. You will hike past multiple waterfalls and finish at a beautiful alpine lake. The total length is 6.4 miles and there is a respectable 1,669 ft of elevation gain as well. This trail can get crowded during the weekends due to it's proximity to population, so we suggest you visit on a weekday.

Location

Go east on I-90 from Seattle and take exit 52, where you'll take FSR 9040 for 1.2 miles and park on the left side of the road. Be aware to not park in the 'third row' of the parking lot, as you'll block those who get there before you. Be considerate and keep the dogs leashed.

Difficulty

NORMAL: This hike doesn't feature any 'technical' terrain, nor does it have an extraneous amount of elevation change. Though there are steeper parts that you'll the burn on, we recommend this hike for anyone who is confident they can walk 8 miles slowly in one day.
