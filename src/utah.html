Reflection Canyon

Description

Reflection Canyon is a remote, unmarked (aside from the occasional cairn). The hike is 20.00mi out and back and has 1,250 ft of elevation gain total. The trail takes you through desolate slot canyons and slickrock and ends up on the 'shores' of Lake Powell.

Location

You'll need a 4x4 to access the trailhead for this hike. Located near Escalante, the Hole in the Rock road is unpaved and unmaintained. The trailhead is a total of 50 miles down the road- be cautious and include a spare tire and compressor in your kit in case of emergency.

Difficulty

HARD: This hike is not for the hobbyist. While the elevation gain doesn't seem substantial, the potential heat and distance of the hike can be staggeringly exhausting for the average person. The hike requires you to keep GPS location at all times.

The trailhead for White Pine Lake is located at Tony's Grove in Logan Canyon. It's located near Garden City and Logan, the former of which is a very popular destination for tourism and would make a good stop if you are travelling this way.

Difficulty

NORMAL: This hike doesn't feature any 'technical' terrain, nor does it have an extraneous amount of elevation change. Though there are steeper parts that you'll the burn on, we recommend this hike for anyone who is confident they can walk 8 miles slowly in one day.
